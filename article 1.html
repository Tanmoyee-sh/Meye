What is Menstruation?

Menstruation, aka having period is when blood and tissue from your uterus comes out of your vagina. It is a normal process that females go through every month as their bodies prepare themselves for potential pregnancy. It is a part of the monthly menstrual cycle (regular cycling of hormones) that occurs in the female reproductive system. This makes pregnancy possible. Medically, the periodic blood that flows as a discharge from the uterus is menstruation. Also called menorrhea, the time during which menstruation occurs is referred to as menses.

What are the signs and symptoms of menstruation?

Menstruation usually start between age 11 and 14 and continue until menopause at about age 51. They usually last from three to five days. Beside the bleeding, other signs and symptoms of menstruation may include

  • Headache,
  • Fatigue,
  • Acne,
  • Abdominal pain,
  • Mood swings,
  • Bloating,
  • Food cravings,
  • Diarrhoea,
  • Tiredness, etc.

What causes Menstruation?

A period happens because of changes in hormones in the body. Hormones are chemical messengers. The ovaries release the female hormones estrogen and progesterone . These hormones cause the lining of the uterus (or womb) to build up. The built-up lining is ready for a fertilized egg to attach to and start developing. If there is no fertilized egg, the lining breaks down and bleeds. Then the same process happens all over again. It usually takes about a month for the lining to build up, then break down. That is why most girls and women get their periods around once a month.

What causes PMS?

Premenstrual syndrome, or PMS, is a group of emotional and physical symptoms that start before or during the period. These symptoms can include moodiness, sadness, anxiety, bloating, and acne. The symptoms go away after the first few days of a period.
